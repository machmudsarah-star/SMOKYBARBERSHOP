Smoky Barbershop SEO Success Plan

Go from hidden to fully booked – follow each step, tick it off, then move on.

1

Goals & Keyword Plan

Decide what success looks like and which search words you want to own.

Tick your main goals:







  • Write down your top goals. This will decide where you put most of your time (for example extra effort on reviews, or extra content for skin fades).
  • Use a simple keyword tool to see what people actually type into Google around “barber”, “barbershop”, “skin fade”, “kids cut”, “beard trim” and suburbs like Bowen Hills, Fortitude Valley, Newstead and Brisbane. Google Keyword Planner is free inside Google Ads. Log in at ads.google.com, go to Tools & Settings → Keyword Planner and choose “Discover new keywords”. You can also try Ubersuggest for extra ideas: Open Ubersuggest.
  • Build a simple keyword map so each page has a job. Example: Home targets “barbershop Bowen Hills” and “barber near Fortitude Valley”. Skin fade page targets “skin fade Bowen Hills”. Kids page targets “kids haircut Bowen Hills”. Beard page targets “beard trim Brisbane”. Put those phrases in the page title, main heading and first paragraph of each page. You edit titles and descriptions in your website builder’s page SEO settings, and edit headings and text directly on the page. Google’s guide to good titles/snippets: see examples.
2

Technical Setup & Speed

Give Google a clean, fast site so it can crawl and trust your pages.

  • Make sure the site is secure (your address starts with https://). This is usually turned on in your hosting or website builder under “SSL” or “Security”.
  • Use clean, short URLs for your pages, like /skin-fade-bowen-hills instead of long addresses with numbers. In most website builders you can open the page settings and change the “URL” or “slug” field to a short, readable version. For structure tips, see Google’s docs on site structure and URLs: view guide.
  • Create or check your XML sitemap and submit it in Google Search Console. If your platform doesn’t create one, use a free generator such as RNfinity XML Sitemap Generator or PowerMapper, download the file as sitemap.xml, upload it to your site, then submit it in Search Console under Indexing → Sitemaps.
  • Check robots.txt so you are not blocking your important pages (home, services, gallery, blog). In Search Console, use the robots.txt tester or open https://yourdomain.com/robots.txt in your browser to see what’s blocked.
  • Fix broken links and set up redirects so old URLs send people to the right page. Tools like the free Seobility Redirect Checker help you see if redirects work correctly.
  • Improve speed and Core Web Vitals so the site loads fast on mobile. Test each important page in Google PageSpeed Insights: open PSI. It shows Core Web Vitals and suggestions such as compressing images and removing heavy scripts.
3

High‑Performing Website Pages

Turn each page into a clear “sales person” that Google and clients understand.

  • For each important page, set a strong title like “Smoky Barbershop | Skin Fades in Bowen Hills, Brisbane”.
  • Use one main heading (H1) per page with your key phrase, for example “Skin Fade Barber in Bowen Hills”.
  • Add 2–4 short sections with headings explaining: who it’s for, what’s included, how long it takes, how to book.
  • Add clear “Book now” buttons and link to your booking page from every service and blog post.
  • Rename service photos to match the service, for example skin-fade-bowen-hills.jpg, and add helpful alt text.

4

Local, Maps & Reviews

Own your local area on Google Maps so nearby people find you first.

  • Claim and complete your Google Business Profile with correct name, address, phone, website, and opening hours.
  • Add your main services (skin fades, classic cuts, beard trims, kids cuts) inside Google Business Profile.
  • Upload fresh shop and haircut photos every month so your listing looks alive.
  • Ask happy clients to leave a Google review and reply to every review with a quick thank you and your suburb name.
  • Check your details are the same on other places like Apple Maps, Facebook, MapQuest and Yellow Pages.
Open Google Business Profile
5

Content & Authority

Show your skill and build trust with useful posts and good links.

  • Once a month, write a short article answering a real client question (for example “How often should I get a skin fade?”).
  • In each article, mention that you are a barber in Bowen Hills near Fortitude Valley and link to the booking page.
  • Ask local gyms, cafés or community pages if they can list Smoky Barbershop on their website with a link.
  • Share each new post and a photo of the cut on Instagram and Facebook with a link back to your website.
6

Tracking & Continuous Growth

Use the numbers to see what works, then do more of it.

  • In Google Analytics, check which pages bring the most visitors and which ones lead to bookings.
  • In Search Console, look at “Performance → Search results” and see which search words show your site but don’t get many clicks.
  • Improve those pages with better titles, clearer descriptions and stronger photos, then check again in a month.
  • Every 3–6 months, repeat this loop: fix weak pages, add one new useful article, collect more reviews.
